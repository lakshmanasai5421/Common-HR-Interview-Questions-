Q
What are your strengths?
⌄
One of my strengths is that I'm open to feedback and always willing to improve. I don't take feedback personally; instead, I see it as an opportunity to learn, grow, and become better at what I do.
Q
What is your weakness?
⌄
I sometimes take a little extra time initially because I like to understand the task properly and avoid mistakes. But once I'm clear, I complete the work efficiently.
Q
What motivates you?
⌄
I'm motivated by opportunities to learn and grow. Whenever I solve a problem or master a new skill, it gives me confidence and motivates me to do even better.
Q
What makes you different from other candidates?
⌄
I may not have years of experience, but I bring curiosity, adaptability, and a growth mindset. I learn quickly, accept feedback positively, and continuously look for ways to improve. I believe these qualities help me contribute and grow faster.
Q
What if someone criticizes you?
⌄
I don't take criticism personally. I see it as a chance to learn. If someone points out a mistake or suggests a better way of doing something, I take it positively and try to improve.

Situational & Problem-Solving
5 questions
Expand all ↓
Q
How do you manage multiple tasks?
⌄
I manage multiple tasks by prioritizing them based on deadlines and importance. I make a list of what needs to be done first, focus on one task at a time, and keep track of progress to ensure everything is completed on time.
Q
What will you do if you make a mistake in a report?
⌄
If I make a mistake in a report, I would first verify and understand what went wrong. I would immediately inform the concerned person or my manager, correct the mistake as quickly as possible, and make sure to double-check my work to avoid repeating it in the future.
Q
What if you receive multiple urgent tasks?
⌄
If I receive multiple urgent tasks, I would first understand the priority and deadlines of each task. I would complete the most critical task first and communicate with my manager if priorities are unclear. This helps me manage my time effectively and ensure all tasks are completed properly.
Q
What if you don't understand an SOP?
⌄
If I don't understand an SOP, I would first read it carefully and try to understand the process. If I still have doubts, I would ask my manager or team members for clarification before proceeding, because it's important to follow the SOP correctly and avoid mistakes.
Q
You are assigned a task with unclear instructions. What would you do?
⌄
If the instructions are unclear, I would first review the information available and try to understand the task. If I still have doubts, I would ask for clarification rather than making assumptions. I believe clear understanding at the beginning leads to better quality work and fewer errors.

Teamwork & Interpersonal Skills
6 questions
Expand all ↓
Q
What role do you usually take in a team?
⌄
I usually act as the person who keeps things moving. I stay organized, take ownership of my work, and step in to help wherever needed. I believe a successful team isn't just about leading — it's about being reliable and contributing wherever you can.
Q
How do you handle disagreements with team members?
⌄
I handle disagreements by staying calm and discussing the issue openly. I try to understand the other person's perspective and work towards a solution that benefits the team.
Q
What would you do if a teammate wasn't contributing?
⌄
I would talk to them first to understand the reason and see if I can help. If the situation doesn't improve, I would escalate it to my manager while focusing on the team's goals.
Q
How do you build trust within a team?
⌄
I build trust by being reliable, transparent, and respectful. I keep my commitments and support the team whenever possible.
Q
Describe a situation where you had a misunderstanding with someone.
⌄
There was a situation where I misunderstood an instruction and started working on the wrong approach. Once I realized it, I informed my teammate, clarified the requirement, and corrected it quickly. Since then, I've made it a habit to confirm my understanding before proceeding.
Q
What would you do if you disagreed with your manager?
⌄
I don't think disagreement is a bad thing. If I had a different opinion, I would express it professionally with facts and respect. At the same time, I understand that managers have a broader perspective, so I would align with the final decision once it's made.

Background & Work Style

Q
Can you tell us about a period not reflected as full-time experience on your resume?
⌄
After completing my graduation, I used that time to strengthen my skills in SQL, Python, and data science through certifications, projects, and internships. I also focused on improving my practical knowledge and preparing myself for the right opportunity. I now feel confident and ready to contribute effectively in a full-time role.
Q
What did you learn from your projects?
⌄
From my projects, I learned how to work with real-world datasets, identify and solve problems, and adapt when my initial approach didn't work. I also improved my SQL and Python skills, understood the importance of data quality, and realized that collaboration and persistence are just as important as technical knowledge.
Q
Why did you choose AI & Data Science?
⌄
Honestly, at first I was curious about this field because of how data is being used everywhere. But during my projects and internships, I realized that I genuinely enjoy working with data, solving problems, and finding practical solutions. That's when my interest became stronger, and I decided to build my career in this domain.
Q
How do you maintain work-life balance?
⌄
During my internship, there were times when I had multiple tasks and tight deadlines. I planned my work by prioritizing urgent tasks and completing them during office hours. Once my work was done, I disconnected from work and spent time with my family and personal activities. This helped me stay refreshed and maintain my productivity the next day.
Q
You find a process that can be improved. What would you do?
⌄
I believe continuous improvement is important. If I noticed a better way of doing something, I would share my idea respectfully with my manager instead of making changes on my own. If the team agreed, I would help implement it.
